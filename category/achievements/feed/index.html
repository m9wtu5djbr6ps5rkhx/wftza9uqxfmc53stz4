Arbitrage traffic — what it is and why it generates revenue What is traffic arbitrage?Arbitrage traffic — what it is and why it generates revenue What is traffic arbitrage?
Traffic arbitrage is buying and selling traffic (visitors) on internet resources in order to generate income. The main idea is to buy traffic on one platform and direct it to another platform where those visitors can be monetized. Monetization can be achieved through advertising, selling goods or services, or by other means.

What is the main difference between traffic arbitrage and affiliate marketing?

The main difference is that in traffic arbitrage, you buy and direct traffic yourself, trying to capitalize on the difference between your acquisition costs and monetization revenue. In affiliate marketing, you promote other companies’ products or services and earn a commission for every successful sale or action achieved via your referral links.

Types of ad traffic

  • When you’re engaged in traffic arbitrage, it’s important to understand what types of traffic exist. They include:
  • Targeted advertising. This type of traffic consists of visitors you attract through ads that are tailored to a specific audience. Targeted ads help you determine exactly who sees your content.
  • Contextual advertising. This type of traffic comes from search engines when a user searches for specific keywords. It enables you to attract customers who are interested in your offer.
  • Push notifications. These are messages sent to users’ devices through a browser or mobile app. They can be used to drive traffic to your site.
  • SEO (search engine optimization). Here the traffic source is organic search results. Optimizing your content properly enables you to attract visitors from search engines for free.
  • Mailouts. Emails and SMS messages can be effective ways to attract traffic if they’re aimed at the right audience.
  • Blogs. If you have a blog, posts can attract traffic from search engines and social media.
  • Teasers. Teasers are small ads that generate interest and are clickable.

    Traffic sales models
  • When you engage in internet traffic arbitrage, you encounter a variety of traffic sales models that may impact your monetization strategy and profitability. It’s important to understand each of these models and choose the one that best suits your goals and target audience:
  • CPC (cost per click). In this model, you pay for every click visitors make on your ad. This model is based on the number of actual clicks on your ad, which can be beneficial if you’re targeting traffic that tends to take actions on your site.
  • CPM (cost per mille — cost per thousand impressions). You pay for every 1,000 views of your ad, regardless of whether someone clicks on it. This model is useful when your goal is to maximize coverage of your audience and create brand recognition.
  • CPA (cost per action). A cost is incurred only when a specific action—such as purchasing an item, registering, or filling out a form—is performed. CPA allows you to measure the specific results and effectiveness of your campaign. This is especially useful if your goal is to convert users into paying customers or subscribers.
